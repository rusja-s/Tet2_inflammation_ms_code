DMRs are defined in a series of the following steps:

1) Define probes using read position generator covering 50 CpGs tiling windows across the genome

2) Filter out large probes (uninformative for defining gene specifc DMRs)

3) Perform total read count quantification on probes and filter out those with too few (insuffucuent for DNA methylation estimation) and too many (likely alignment artefacts) reads

4) Exclude proes on sex chromosomes and mitochondrial genome (Valid autosomal probes)

5) Quantify DNA methylation percentage for each probes. Quantitation was Methylation feature pipeline quantitation using 'Valid Autosomal probes' with min count of 1 per position, and at least 10 (out of 50) observations per feature, then combining using the Mean

6) Perform Logistic regression analysis p<0.05, Min Obs=20 between following four groups: KO (veh) vs WT (veh); KO (IL-1b) vs WT (IL-1b); IL-1b vs. vehicle (WT); IL-1b vs. vehicle (KO).

7) Filter Hyper- and Hypo- methylated probes subsetting stat significant probes from step 6) with a minimum absolute difference of 10% in each analyzed condition
